Overall, this looks thoughtful and internally consistent, but I noticed a few issues that I'd fix before shipping.

### 1. There's a factual error about Bitcoin

The **About** section says:

> **Launched:** 2015
> **Consensus:** Proof of Stake

Both are incorrect.

Bitcoin should be:

* **Launched:** 2009
* **Consensus:** Proof of Work

Those values currently describe Ethereum more than Bitcoin.

---

### 2. There are conflicting prices

I see several different BTC prices on the same screen:

* Header: **$62,439**
* Spot: **$62,454.21**
* Telemetry Matrix: **$61,848.88**
* EMA: **$62,380.94**

The EMA is fine because it's a different metric, but the two spot prices should generally match unless you clearly label them as coming from different timestamps or candle closes. Otherwise users may think the app is inconsistent.

---

### 3. Volume messaging conflicts

Near the top:

> Volume drying up
> Latest session volume is ~86% below the 7-day average.

Later:

> Participation is strong

Those can both be true if they're measuring different things (e.g. relative volume vs on-chain participation), but the user doesn't know that.

I'd clarify:

* **Exchange volume:** Below average
* **Market participation:** Above baseline

or rename "Participation" to something more specific.

---

### 4. Compression vs strong volume

You describe:

* volatility compression
* calm volatility
* volume drying up

Those naturally go together.

If "Participation is strong" is based on a different metric, consider surfacing that distinction.

---

### 5. State engine is believable

This section feels coherent:

* Trend → Positive
* Flow → Negative
* Risk → Neutral
* Participation → Positive

That naturally leads to:

> Agreement: Low

which is exactly what I'd expect when the clusters disagree.

---

### 6. Reconstruction wording

> Bitcoin is trading 0.9% beneath its 20-day EMA.

Good.

> velocity is cooling down rapidly below historical norms.

I'd capitalize "Velocity" since it's the first word after the period.

Also consider:

> Momentum is cooling rapidly below historical norms.

It's slightly clearer than "velocity."

---

### 7. Signal breakdown vs State Engine

One thing stood out.

State Engine:

> Trend Positive

Signal Breakdown:

> Trend is weakening — bearish

Those aren't necessarily contradictory:

* the **trend state** can still be positive,
* while **recent trend direction** is weakening.

But I'd make that distinction explicit.

For example:

* **Trend State:** Positive
* **Current Trend:** Weakening

or

> Long-term trend remains constructive, though short-term trend has turned bearish.

---

### 8. Timeline

I like:

> Timeline reads oldest → newest across the last 7 daily sessions.

Very clear.

---

### 9. Expected move

```
Expected move
$60,879 → $64,029
```

paired with

```
1σ of trailing 14-day daily returns × √3
```

is excellent. It explains that it's a statistical range rather than a prediction.

---

## Overall

From a quantitative market perspective, I'd give it **9/10**.

The main improvements I'd make are:

* Correct the Bitcoin metadata (2009, Proof of Work).
* Unify or timestamp spot prices.
* Clarify the difference between "volume" and "participation."
* Better distinguish long-term trend state from short-term trend direction.

The overall presentation feels more like a professional terminal than a typical crypto app. The sections build on one another instead of repeating the same information, and the explanations strike a good balance between technical detail and readability.
